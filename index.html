About

I am a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at Pennsylvania State University, working under Prof. Adri C.T. van Duin — the pioneer of the ReaxFF reactive force field. My research sits at the intersection of computational chemistry, materials science, and molecular engineering.

My current work focuses on developing and applying ReaxFF force fields for polymer systems — particularly PDMS (polydimethylsiloxane) — studying anionic ring-opening polymerization (AROP) mechanisms at the atomic scale. I also investigate gold nanoparticle wettability and surface chemistry using reactive molecular dynamics.

Before Penn State, I graduated from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) with a B.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering, ranking 28th out of 188 students with a CGPA of 3.81/4.00. My undergraduate thesis analyzed the mechanical performance of crumpled graphene using molecular dynamics simulations.

Current Research

Polymer Chemistry · ReaxFF
PDMS Formation via Anionic Ring-Opening Polymerization

Developing ReaxFF force field parameters for PDMS to simulate its formation through AROP mechanisms at the atomic level — capturing bond formation, chain growth, and termination events.

Ongoing
Nanomaterials · Surface Science
Gold Nanoparticle Wettability

Investigating the wettability and surface chemistry of gold nanoparticles using reactive molecular dynamics — understanding how surface functionalization affects contact angle and adhesion at the nanoscale.

Ongoing
Force Field Development
ReaxFF Parameterization for Si–O–C–H Systems

Systematic parameterization of ReaxFF force fields using DFT-based training sets for Si–O–C–H chemistry relevant to silicone polymer systems and organosilicon compounds.

Ongoing
Undergraduate Thesis · BUET 2023
Mechanical Performance of Crumpled Graphene

Investigated fracture toughness, porosity effects, and strain energy storage in crumpled graphene via LAMMPS MD simulations. Found a critical crumpling threshold of 14.7 Å for fracture toughness reversal.
